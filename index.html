Western Juniper

(Juniper communis)

Trees

CC BY-SA

Summary

Juniperus communis, the common juniper, is a species of small tree or shrub in the cypress family Cupressaceae. An evergreen conifer, it has the largest geographical range of any woody plant, with a circumpolar distribution throughout the cool temperate Northern Hemisphere. Juniperus communis is very variable in form, ranging from 10 meters (33 ft)—rarely 16 m (52 ft)—tall to a low, often prostrate spreading shrub in exposed locations

Plant Description

  • Plant Type: Tree
  • Height: 33.0 ft (10.1 m)
  • Width: 9.0 - 12.0 ft (2.7 - 3.7 m)
  • Growth Rate: Slow
  • Flower Color: Brown, Yellow, Green
  • Flowering Season: Spring
  • Leaf Rentention: Evergreen

Growth Requirements

  • Sun: Full Sun, Part Shade
  • Water: Medium, High
  • Drainage: Fast, Medium

Natural Habitat

Gravel, rocky, and sandy soils; dry and damp places; near beaches; on hills and bluffs; in pine forests.

Other Names

Common Names: Scarlet Maple, Soft Maple, Swamp Maple, Rot-Ahorn, Rotahorn, Plaine, Plaine Rouge, Érable Rouge, Acero Rosso, ödlön

Scientific Names: Acer rubrum, Acer rubrum f. rubrum, Acer rubrum var. tomentosum, Acer sanguineum, Acer rubrum f. breviramusculum, Acer rubrum var. pallidiflorum, Rufacer rubrum, Acer rubrum f. sanguineum, Acer rubrum f. tomentosum, Acer rubrum var. columnare
